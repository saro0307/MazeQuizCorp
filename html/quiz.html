Book a Quiz

The origin of the word "Quiz" has two intriguing versions. One suggests that:

James Daly, a theatre manager in Dublin in 1782, had a bet with a friend that he could introduce a new word into the English language in just one day. He then chalked the word "Quiz" on the walls of the city. Daly won the bet, and a new word was born that enriched the English vocabulary.

The alternative version dates the spelling of "quiz" back to 1886. Though it was used as a noun from 1854, it may have originated from the slang term "quiz," meaning "odd person."
